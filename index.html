Reese's origin

The Reese's Peanut Butter Cup was invented in 1928 by a former Hershey employee named Harry Burnett(H.B) Reese. He had had a total of 16 children when the Reese's Peanut Butter Cup this was due to a costumers of his in Hurrisburg, Pennslyvannia he had reported a supply problems with another onfectioner who made candy consisting pf peanut butter covered with chocolate. Later down the line the company was packing 120 individually wrapped pieces per box that sold for a penny per cup. They would come up with slogan "Made in Chocolate Town, So They Must Be Good." The Hershey Company supplied HB Reese with the chocolate coating for his candy and had kept a close relationship thoughtout his candy ventures. In 1956 H.B Reese would die at 76 due to a heart attack leaving the business to his six sons but would evenually sold it to Hershey in 1963 thus having the peanut butter cups we all love today.
